Traditional Music and Dance

Selialia Dance

A traditional Sesotho dance and game for young Basotho men and women in Lesotho

Lesiba

A mouth-resonated bow with a calabash amplifier.

Setolo-tolo

Bass string instrument played with fingers.

Thomo

One-string violin made from a tin can and horse-hair.

sekhankula music

Bass string instrument played with fingers.

Famo music

A contemporary Sesotho music genre that features an accordion and
an oil can drum, originating from the 1920s migrant worker culture. .

Mohobelo

An energetic and physically demanding dance performed by men,
featuring dramatic strides, slides, and high leaps.
It is often performed for evening entertainment and includes a leader
who recites praise poetry.

Ndlamo

Originally a Zulu war dance, Ndlamo was integrated into Basotho culture,
particularly by the Matebele people. It is a vigorous,
heavy-footed stamping dance performed by men and boys,
often with decorated sticks and ankle rattles.

Tsilo

A circular dance performed by women to express the unity and love within the community

Molilietsane

Not a dance itself, but a form of ululation,
or shouts of applause and encouragement, that accompanies women's dances like Mokhibo.

Lesokoana

More of a game and ritual than a formal dance,
it was traditionally played by women and girls during droughts to invoke rain..

Litolobonya

A dance performed by young Basotho women as a rite of passage,
celebrating femininity, grace, and strength..

Sotho Hip-hop

A popular modern genre that includes well-known artists
like Malome Vector, Wiseman Molefe, and Ntate Stunna.

AfroPop, Jazz, and Deep House

Common music styles in Lesotho, often influenced by South African artists.

Lekolulo

A traditional flute played by herding boys.

Lesiba

Considered Lesotho's national instrument, it is a "stringed-wind" ,
instrument consisting of a quill and string attached to a hardwood board,
producing sound when air is blown across it. It is also known as the "herd boy's gramophone".

Setolo-tolo

An extended jaw harp that men play using their mouths.

Thomo

A stringed instrument traditionally played by Basotho women.

Sekupu and Moropa

Drums often used in conjunction with music and dance at community events.

Serobele

An example of vocalized sounds that are categorized under the "voiced" group of instruments.

Gum boot dance

This style developed among migrant mine laborers, reflecting their experiences.

Khooara-Khooara

An example of vocalized sounds that are categorized
under the "voiced" group of instruments.

Letjoli

A vibrant female dance that involves a circle of women clapping and singing as individuals take turns dancing in the center.
